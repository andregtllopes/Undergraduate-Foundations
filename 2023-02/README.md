# Semester 2023/2: Strengthening Foundations & Institutional Growth

The second semester of 2023 represented a significant expansion of the technical curriculum, moving from introductory theory to applied computation, physical experimentation, and institutional leadership.

## 📌 Overview
This period was marked by the consolidation of core engineering principles through a heavy academic load involving multivariable calculus, classical mechanics, and the beginning of my journey in computer programming. Additionally, I began my active participation in student representation by joining the Aerospace Engineering Student Union (GRAERO).

## 🛠️ Key Academic Focus
* **Multivariable Calculus & Optimization (MAT039):** Mastery of gradients, differentiability in $\mathbb{R}^n$, and Lagrange multipliers—essential for understanding optimization in machine learning.
* **Programming & Algorithmic Logic (DCC001):** Introduction to software development, focusing on modularization and data structures (vectors/matrices).
* **Classical Mechanics & Experimental Validation (FIS065/FIS151):** Deepening the understanding of particle dynamics and conservation laws, supported by rigorous laboratory verification.
* **Metrology & Statistical Inference (EMA257/EST031):** Development of technical rigor in measurement systems and the use of probability for data-driven engineering decisions.

## 🚀 Highlighted Project
* **[Linear Systems Solver](./Numerical-Methods-Linear-Systems/):** My very first technical software, implemented in Python to solve lower triangular systems ($Lx = b$). This project reflects the "First-Principles" approach by implementing the underlying math before using high-level libraries.

## 🏛️ Institutional Engagement
* **GRAERO (Board of Sports):** In October 2023, I joined the Student Union, focusing on logistics, team management, and student integration, which helped develop essential soft skills in leadership and communication.

## 🧠 Perspective
By the end of this semester, the synergy between mathematics, physics, and computation became clear. The ability to model a physical phenomenon and translate it into a functional algorithm became a primary interest, setting the stage for future research in AI and numerical simulations.
