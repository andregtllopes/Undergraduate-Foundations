# Semester 2024/1: Analytical Expansion & Professional Immersion

The first semester of 2024 was defined by a strategic balance between an intensive academic workload—centered on theoretical physics and advanced calculus—and the beginning of a high-impact professional trajectory. 

## 📌 Overview
This period marked a transition into the study of physical fields and dynamic systems. Academically, the focus was on mastering the mathematical tools required to model complex engineering phenomena. Professionally, this semester marked my entry into the **Management Consulting** and **Junior Enterprise** ecosystem through AEROJR.

## 🛠️ Key Academic Focus
* **Differential Equations & Laplace Transforms (MAT015):** Mastery of ODEs and power series solutions. This provides the mathematical backbone for future control systems and flight dynamics modeling.
* **Vector Calculus & Field Theory (MAT002):** Deep dive into double/triple integrals and the Theorems of Gauss and Stokes. These are the "First-Principles" for understanding fluid flux and electromagnetic potentials.
* **Electromagnetism, Oscillations & Waves (FIS069/FIS087):** Study of Maxwell’s equations and wave behavior, essential for aerospace instrumentation and communication systems.
* **Materials Science (EMA247):** Understanding the structure-property relationship of aerospace alloys (Al, Ti, Mg) and composites, focusing on stress-strain analysis and failure criteria.
* **Epistemology & Philosophy of Science (FIL029):** Critical analysis of scientific knowledge construction, adding a layer of rigorous logical thinking to technical problem-solving.

## 💼 Professional Immersion: AEROJR.
* **Trainee Program (Job Rotation):** In April 2024, I was accepted into **AEROJR.** (Aerospace Engineering Junior Enterprise). I underwent a job rotation through Marketing, Sales, Projects, and Management departments.
* **Business Vision:** This experience allowed me to bridge the gap between pure engineering and market strategy, leading to my eventual placement as a Marketing Associate.
* **Soft Skills & Quality:** Intensive training in Non-Violent Communication (NVC), feedback culture, and Quality Management Systems (SGQ).

## 🏛️ Institutional Engagement
* **GRAERO (Board of Sports):** Continued tenure in the Student Union, managing logistics and student integration while balancing a 360-hour academic semester.
